How did I get here?

By Avia ben Uriah · Tel Aviv · Spring 2026

I'm from Tel Aviv, born and raised, although my mother is American, so English came first and Hebrew showed up shortly after, slightly ignored. I grew up translating between the two, badly at first, then well enough to notice when a phrase that sounds great in one language sounds insane in the other. That noticing turned out to be a huge advantage. I write for a living now, and most of what I do is still translating: a founder's voice into a brand, a paper into a one-liner, a guest's answer into the question they actually meant to be asked.

I loved movies before I could explain why — watching the same scenes back, looking for the seam where a line earned its place or didn't. I started writing things down in middle school and never stopped. I learned early that the right line in the right language could make a room laugh, or shut it up. I've been chasing that ever since.

From 8200 to screenwriting, naturally.

I served in 8200 as a data analyst, which is the part of the resume that gets people to lean forward, and then I went and majored in screenwriting at the Steve Tisch School of Film and Television at TAU, which is the part where they lean back. I wanted to figure out what I actually liked before I optimized for what I was good at. The army taught me to find the signal in a wall of noise. Film school taught me that the signal is usually in subtext. I'm in the middle of an MFA in screenwriting now, still chasing both. Both turn out to be useful.

One muscle, many surfaces.

I took a copywriting course expecting it to feel like a detour. It didn't. I noticed the same instincts I used in a screenplay were doing the work for a brand, a product page, a podcast intro, a video script. The form changes, yet the job underneath doesn't. Bong Joon-ho, paraphrasing Scorsese at the Oscars, said the most personal is the most creative. I keep that line close. The materials I write at school and the work I do for clients are the same muscle: a specific voice, a specific room, a specific person on the other side of the page.

What I'm looking for.

Anything that needs writing, producing, vision and build — from brand and narrative roles at companies finding their voice to content developer on shows, series, podcasts, and publications. But most importantly, I'm looking for a place to grow with and root myself in. I'm here to keep learning.

And if you've made it this far, I also make an excellent barista.
