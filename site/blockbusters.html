Feature No. 03 — Podcasting

Building Blockbusters

A branded Web3 podcast covering blockchain, crypto marketing, and the future of decentralized tech.

producer & Content manager · MarketAcross · 2025–2026

Role
Founding producer. Owned the show end to end, focusing on format, page building and distribution.
Scope
Format design, guest briefing, Riverside production, editing direction, copy across every surface, landing page, graphics direction, publishing schedule.
Words
All page copy, episode descriptions, CTAs and brand voice for the show.
Live at
marketacross.com/podcast

MarketAcross is one of Web3’s largest PR agencies, including end-to-end marketing for blockchain companies, with 300+ clients across the industry’s biggest names. The firm had spent years building the relationships, the experience and the track record. What it didn’t have was a platform of its own.

One of those goals was a podcast: a way to host the conversations the brand was already part of, in a format the company owned and could grow inside. The brief started with my hiring: goals, format and production were mine to shape.

I shaped the show around the audience that mattered to the brand: founders, CMOs and operators inside the industry, plus the journalists covering them. Then I worked on the voice. Confident without being loud. An expert POV from a team that had lived through more than one cycle and knew how to survive each. A show about PR, marketing and growth in Web3, not delivered in a PR tone.

From zero to on-air

The format: a long-form discussion show, 30–40 minutes per episode. Produced on Riverside, published on YouTube from day one, wrapped in a cover system that read as Web3 and tech rather than agency PR. I wrote the briefs and directed the edit on tone and pacing.

Once a few episodes were running, the show needed a home that matched its standard — a curated space where the catalogue could live, and a way to position Blockbusters as a legitimate podcast series, not a side experiment. I researched the references, briefed the design team and led the page through. The landing page became the brand in its quietest form. Every design decision was a copy decision first — and the show, the page and the audio all held to one voice.

Visit the show → marketacross.com/podcast

What the brand gained

A new owned surface, in a new format. A YouTube channel, an episode index and a landing page where there had been none — three properties the company could keep filling, indefinitely, in its own voice.

Distribution through the guests. Founders, CMOs and journalists shared their episodes on Twitter and LinkedIn. Every release put the MarketAcross name in front of audiences our clients had spent years building.

A platform that kept running. I left a format guide, brief templates, a guest pipeline and a visual system. The show kept producing after I left, on the system I’d proudly built.

What I took from this

Blockbusters taught me to trust my instincts. I set the standard for the show: branding guidelines, format, design, working with editors, all to bring my vision through. By the time I left, the team was running it on its own. Creating something that lasts, that’s the biggest achievement I could ask for.
