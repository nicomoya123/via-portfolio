Feature No. 04 — Content & social

B2B content marketing: creating the playbook.

Building brand voice, presence, and authority for one of Web3's largest PR agencies.

founding content & social hire · MarketAcross · 2025–2026

B2B content marketing was new ground for me, and at the same time, it wasn’t. I’d come up writing for screen, for stage, for clients across other industries. I knew how to build narrative, write to a brief, hold a voice across hundreds of pieces, decide what a reader needed to feel by the end of a paragraph.

MarketAcross hired me as its first dedicated content and social hire — to build a brand voice for the agency itself. The mandate was wide open: blog, social, thought leadership, every surface where the company spoke for itself.

So I built the operation before I built the voice. I set up analytics, benchmarked us against the agencies we competed with, audited the topics we could credibly own and the ones we couldn’t yet, and committed to a publishing cadence I could actually keep.

The first pulse: social

Social was where I could move fastest. I took LinkedIn and X end-to-end and built a publishing rhythm across four streams: event coverage, educational content, client success stories, and weekly series. Most of it I produced end-to-end — copy, content editing, video direction, carousel and image design. LinkedIn grew an average of 40+ net new followers a month, and I started a monthly analytics meeting to benchmark against competitors and decide what was working, what wasn't, and what to retire.

building a content engine

From there my focus was building a 360 content engine — blog, social, podcast, thought leadership, event coverage, evergreen SEO, AEO and GEO — all running off the same brand voice. I designed a process for getting the expertise out of the people who held it: half-hour interviews with account managers, strategists and leadership, turned into 1,200-word pieces drafted by me and bylined to whoever earned it. The editorial standard was mine; the expertise was theirs.
